Rotate the cadaver to visualize each item thoroughly, and identify the aperatures of the pelvic wall.

These include the greater sciatic foramen, the lesser sciatic foramen and the obturator canal.
In the image below, note the structures that penetrate the pelvic wall through these aperatures.

The Lesser sciatic foramen is the only entry for vessels and nerves to the perineum. All the other apertures allow entry from the pelvis to the leg and gluteal areas.
