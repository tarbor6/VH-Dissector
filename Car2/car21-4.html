Now we will build the components of the left side of the heart.

Add the posterior view of the left atrium.
Add the pulmonary veins.
Rotate to an anterior view and add the interatrial septum.
Add the mitral valve.

Why is this valve called the "mitral" valve?

It is the shape of a Catholic bishops hat, which is called a miter.
Add the aortic valve. To see the aortic valve you have to remove the "brown colored" left ventricle.

This "brown colored" object will have to be removed in the next steps to see the valvular structures.
Add the interventricular septum and rotate to see the relationship of these two valves to the ventricular septum.
Add the ascending aorta.
Tap on image to enlarge
Briefly trace the blood flow from the pulmonary veins through the mitral valve and the aortic valve to the aorta.
