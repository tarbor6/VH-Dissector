Respiration (inspiration and expiration) is performed with the following muscles:

Inspiration:

Tap on image to enlarge
Add the diaphragm which contracts and creates negative pressure.
Add the internal intercostal muscles.
Add the external intercostal muscles.
Tap on image to enlarge

The intercostal muscles contract and stiffen the rib cage to counter negative pressure, along with lifting and rotating ribs 2 through 10 to increase the thoracic volume.

Think of the movement of the intercostal muscles as a combination of a pump lever and a bucket handle maneuver to increase thoracic volume.

The lungs passively inflate during this part of the cycle.

Forced inspiration is accomplished by adding the following muscles:

Add the scalene muscles which lift the first two ribs.
Add the sternocleidomastoid which along with the clavicle attaches to the sternum and lifts the sternum outward to increase chest volume. Thereby increasing the pump lever action.

Neck and back muscles combine to elevate the pectoral girdle and thereby increase the cross sectional area of the thorax. Extending the back increases the rostral-caudal length.

Upper respiratory tract muscles relax and decrease upper airway resistance.
