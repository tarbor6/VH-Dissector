Expiration:

Passive (quiet) expiration occurs when all the above muscles just relax.

However, expiration can be forced and then the following muscles come into use:

Tap on image to enlarge
Add the abdominal muscles (including the internal and external obliques and the rectus abdominus muscles) which increase abdominal pressure and force the diaphragm upward.
Add the subsets of the internal and external intercostal muscles which reduce anterior,posterior and transverse diameters of the thorax. This is particularly important when coughing..

The neck and back muscles relax and therefore reduce the cross sectional area of the thorax. Leaning forward also reduces the rostral caudal length. (Think of a sneeze)
