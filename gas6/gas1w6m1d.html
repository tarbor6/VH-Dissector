What is the major blood supply to the body of the pancreas?

The splenic artery and its small branches, of which the greater pancreatic artery is one

What is the blood supply to the head of the pancreas?

The pancreaticoduodenal arteries.

Why does this make sense embryologically?

This is because of the anterior (ventral) pancreatic bud rotates and joins the posterior (dorsal) bud of the pancreas. Therefore, each bud has a separate blood supply.
