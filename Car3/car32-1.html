CAR PART 3 Module 2

OBJECTIVES:

2.3.1   Follow the course of a primary, secondary, and tertiary bronchus to at least one bronchopulmonary segment.

2.3.2   Follow the course of blood flow in the pulmonary arteries and the pulmonary veins on the hilar surface to at least one bronchopulmonary segment.

2.3.3   Describe the flow of lymph from each lung and the location of lymph nodes associated with the lung (whether or not they are seen on the images).

2.3.4   Trace the lymphatic drainage of the thoracic walls, pleura, and lungs.

READINGS:

Gray's Anatomy for Students (Fourth Edition): Pages: 125-247. See Green Highlighted sections for clinical correlation.

SECTRA TABLE WORK: Page 1 of 4

Begin with the trachea.
Add the left and right main bronchi.

From here the bronchi divide into the lobar and then segmental bronchi.

We will investigate each lobe separately.

Using the Highlight or Draw tool, identify the location of the carina. Carina means keel (as in a ship). If you look at the carina with a bronchoscope (See picture below), there is a ridge on the interior of the bifrucation that looks like a keel of a boat.
